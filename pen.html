Digital Art

Digital art portraits are portraits done with digital media and equipment such as drawing tablets and software. They can range from very realistic renderings to stylized or abstract depictions, and they can be created in a variety of styles and techniques.

Digital art portraits have various advantages over traditional portraiture. They let artists to create fast and efficiently, experiment with numerous approaches and styles, and readily make alterations or adjustments to their work.

Digital art portraits can be quickly shared online or printed in high resolution, making them more accessible to a wider audience.

Adobe Photoshop, Corel Painter, and Procreate are among popular software programmes for generating digital art portraits. For artists interested in learning more about producing digital art portraits, there are several tutorials and tools available online.

Pen Art

Pen art portrait is a style of artwork in which the artist creates a portrait of a person with a pen or multiple pens. Pen art portraits can be done in a variety of forms such as realistic, abstract, or comical. They can be made with a variety of pens, including ballpoint pens, felt-tip pens, and brush pens.

Pen art portraits can be difficult to draw due to the level of detail required to convey the subject's likeness. To make a realistic portrait, you must master anatomy, shading, and proportions.

Pen art portraits can be done on a range of different surfaces, such as paper, canvas, or even wood. They can also be mixed with other mediums to give colour and texture to the portrait, such as watercolour or acrylic paint.

Overall, pen art portraits can be a beautiful and one-of-a-kind method to record a person's likeness, but they require patience, expertise, and attention to detail to create.
