watercolour Art

Watercolour is a unique medium that requires diluting paint with water to create a translucent and fluid look on paper.

Watercolour portraits can be done in a range of styles, ranging from loose and expressive to meticulous and realistic. The technique is gradually layering up layers of paint, beginning with a faint wash of colour and adding features and depth as you go.

Watercolour portraiture takes skills and practise, but it can be a highly gratifying and fun process. To obtain the desired appearance, use high-quality watercolour paper and paints, as well as experiment with different brushes and techniques.

Bharatanatyam Portrait It'll blow your mind.

Bharatanatyam is a traditional Indian dance genre that began in the Tamil Nadu state.This portrait of a Bharatanatyam dancer is made with black paint on white paper. This is accomplished by first sketching the shape of the dancer's face and body, then filling in the details with black paint, paying close attention to the delicate details of the costume and jewels.
